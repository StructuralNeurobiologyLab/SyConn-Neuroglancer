Project

SyConn-Neuroglancer viewer helps to visualize EM volumetric data (3D segmentation and raw volumes). It is based on a fork of the original google/neuroglancer project developed and maintained by Jeremy Maitin-Shepard. It is capable of rendering line-segment based skeletons and triangular mesh representations. Additionally, it can also render organelle meshes that follow the same triangular representation.

The datasets used by the viewer are in the Knossos format which allows reading the data chunk-wise and forwarding to the Neuroglancer client. The skeleton and mesh representations are made available by SyConn, modified to the precomputed format for rendering. As part of the rendering options, one can change the visual look and feel of these cell objects directly from the browser.

The viewer also supports filtering the cells based on some properties. Currently, we support filtering by the cell type, number of mitochondria within a cell and the size of the cell. Additionally, one can request the synaptic partner of a cell based on the largest synapse area.

The Team

This project is currently developed in Prof. Winfried Denk's department Electrons - Photons - Neurons at the Max Planck Institute of Neurobiology in Martinsried, Germany.
